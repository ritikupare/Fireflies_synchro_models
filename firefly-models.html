How the model works
Each firefly has its own natural flash frequency (ωᵢ). They continuously adjust their phase based on what neighbors are doing — pulling toward the mean phase. Above a critical coupling Kc, spontaneous synchrony emerges from the crowd with no leader.
Each firefly charges an internal potential x ∈ [0,1]. When it reaches 1, it fires (flashes) and resets to 0. Each flash gives all neighbors a forward nudge ε. The concave rise function guarantees that nudged oscillators always move closer together — making cascade synchrony inevitable.
